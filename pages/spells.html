Spellcasting Overview — your core casting stats, spell slots, and class resources all in one place.

Spellcasting Ability — choose the ability your class uses: INT (Wizard, Artificer), WIS (Cleric, Druid, Ranger), or CHA (Bard, Paladin, Sorcerer, Warlock). This drives the two auto-calculated fields below.

Spell Save DC — auto: 8 + Proficiency Bonus + spellcasting modifier. Enemies roll against this when they resist your spells. Type to override (e.g. magic items that boost it).

Spell Attack Bonus — auto: Proficiency Bonus + spellcasting modifier. Added to d20 rolls for spells that require an attack roll. Type to override.

Caster Type — Prepared (Cleric/Druid/Wizard prepare a subset each day), Known (Bard/Sorcerer/Ranger know a fixed list), or Spellbook (Wizard tracking their book).

Spells Prepared — your maximum number of prepared spells. Usually: spellcasting modifier + character level (half for half-casters). Reference only — doesn't enforce a limit.

Spell Slots — click "+ Add Spell Slot Type" to add slots. Each slot has a name (e.g. "1st Level"), a max count, and a rest type (Long Rest resets most, Short Rest for Warlock Pact Magic). Use the +/− buttons during play to spend and recover slots. Drag the ⠿ handle to reorder. Click the pencil icon to edit name, max, or reset type.

Custom Resources — for class features that aren't spell slots: Ki points, Sorcery Points, Channel Divinity, Bardic Inspiration, Superiority Dice, etc. Same +/− tracking with long or short rest resets.

Prepared Spells & Cantrips — a quick-reference table of everything you have marked as prepared or known, grouped by spell level.

This view is read-only — it pulls from your Cantrips and Spells lists below. To appear here a spell must be marked Prepared in its details, or be a cantrip (which are always available).

Columns: Name, Level, Casting Time, Range, Effect/Damage, and a View button to see full details. Use this table during play for a fast overview without scrolling through the full spell lists.

To mark a spell as prepared: find it in the Cantrips or Spells section, open its card and tick the Prepared checkbox.

Favorites — your most-used cantrips and spells pinned at the top for fast access during combat.

To favourite a spell: open its card in the Cantrips or Spells section and click the ★ star icon. It will appear here instantly.

Favourites are split into two columns — cantrips on the left, levelled spells on the right. They show a compact summary: name, school, casting time, and range. Click any card to see full details.

Use this section during combat so you don't have to scroll — just glance here for the spells you cast most often.

Cantrips — your at-will spells. Cantrips never use spell slots and can be cast any number of times.

+ Add Cantrip — fill in a blank form with all details manually.

Import Cantrips — search the built-in spell database (482 spells) and add directly. Much faster than manual entry.

Filter dropdown — show All, Prepared, Known, or Favorites only.

Search bar — live search by name as you type. Stacks with the dropdown filter.

Each cantrip card shows name, school, and casting info. Expand it to see the full description. Use the ★ to favourite, the pencil to edit, or the bin to delete.

Clear All Cantrips — requires 3 clicks to confirm. Use only when starting fresh.

Spells — your levelled spells (1st–9th level). These cost spell slots to cast.

+ Add Spell — fill in manually: name, level, school, casting time, range, components, duration, damage/effect, save type, attack roll, ritual, concentration, and description.

Import Spells — search the built-in database and add with one click. Includes all PHB spells plus many supplements.

Filters — narrow by level (1st–9th), school (Evocation, Illusion, etc.), and status (Prepared, Known, Ritual, Favorites). All three stack together.

Search bar — live search by name, stacks with all filters.

Prepared checkbox — tick on a spell's card to mark it prepared. Prepared spells appear in the Prepared Spells table at the top.

Concentration / Ritual — flagged on the card as a reminder. Concentration means you can only maintain one at a time.

Clear All Spells — requires 3 clicks. Cannot be undone.

Spell Notes — free-text area for anything spell-related that doesn't fit in a card.

Good uses: homebrew spell modifications, upcast notes (e.g. "Fireball deals +1d6 per slot above 3rd"), DM rulings on specific spells, material component costs, or reminders about concentration interactions.

Saves automatically with your character.
